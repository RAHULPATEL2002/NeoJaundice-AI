How to Use NeoJaundice AI

Follow these best practices to ensure the most accurate AI screening results for the newborn.

📸 Capturing Photos

1. Lighting Matters

Use natural, indirect daylight whenever possible. Avoid harsh fluorescent lights or strong yellow bulbs which can distort color analysis.

2. Skin Photo (Forehead/Chest)

The skin photo should be taken from approximately 15-20cm away. Ensure there are no shadows or glare on the skin surface.

3. Eye/Sclera Photo

Gently open the baby's eyelid if needed. The AI specifically looks at the white part of the eye (sclera). Ensure the eye is in sharp focus.

📑 Interpretation

Classification Action Required
Normal Routine monitoring. No immediate action needed.
Mild Monitor closely. Ensure frequent breastfeeding.
Moderate Consult a pediatrician. Phototherapy may be required.
Severe Urgent: Seek immediate medical treatment at a hospital.
Critical Safety Check: If the baby is lethargic, not feeding, or has a high-pitched cry, seek medical help immediately regardless of the AI screening result.
